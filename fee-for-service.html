Expanded Support for Our Community

Counselling for Everyone

Desert Sun is pleased to offer Fee for Service Counselling in addition to the free counselling we provide under our Ministry of Justice contract. These private sessions are open to anyone seeking support — not just those who fall within our mandated programs.

Free Counselling (Under Mandate)

Free counselling is available for women, children, and men who have experienced abuse, violence, or assault under our Ministry of Justice contract. The PEACE Program also supports children ages 3–18 affected by family conflict. Learn about our free programs →

Fee for Service Counselling

For individuals and couples seeking support outside the scope of our mandated programs — grief, anxiety, depression, relationship challenges, stress, and the ongoing work of building resilience and coping skills.

How It Works

Process & Costs

We’ve made booking simple. Review our counsellors below, choose one whose approach fits your needs, and book directly online. Some counsellors offer a free introductory consultation so you can find the right match.

Step 1 — Choose Your Counsellor

Online

Review the biographies of our professional counsellors below and choose one that aligns with your needs and preferences. Be mindful of the designations your extended benefits provider recognizes if coverage is important to you.

Step 2 — Free Consultation (Optional)

No Obligation

Some of our counsellors offer a free introductory consultation call. Establishing a trusting relationship with your counsellor is important, and this short call helps you decide whether the fit feels right before booking a full session.

Step 3 — Book Your Session

$125 / hour

One-hour counselling sessions are $125 and can be booked online through our secure OWL Practice booking portal. Each of our counsellors has their own booking calendar so you can pick a time that works for you.

  • One-hour sessions: $125
  • Secure online booking via OWL Practice
  • In person, online, or by phone (where offered)
  • Check with your benefits provider for possible coverage
