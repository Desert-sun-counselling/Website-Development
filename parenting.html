Parenting Supports

Parenting is one of the most important and challenging roles there is. Desert Sun offers free, evidence-based programs to strengthen families and support parents and caregivers at every stage.

For All Families

Our parenting programs welcome all caregivers — biological parents, foster parents, grandparents, and anyone raising a child. You do not need to be in crisis to access support.

Always Free

Every parenting program at Desert Sun is provided free of charge. No referral is required. Simply call or email us and we will help you find the right program for your family.

Group Program

Nobody’s Perfect

Nobody’s Perfect is a nationally recognized parenting program for parents and caregivers of children from birth to age 5. The program takes place in small group settings where parents can learn, share experiences, and build confidence alongside others in similar situations.

Facilitated by a trained Desert Sun staff member, Nobody’s Perfect covers topics like child development, safety, behaviour, nutrition, and the day-to-day realities of early parenting. The program is designed to be practical, welcoming, and judgment-free.

Program Details
For parents and caregivers of children ages 0–5
Small group format — typically 6–8 participants
Sessions held over several weeks
Call to find out about current scheduling: (250) 498-2538
Flagship Parenting Program

DS Cooks

DS Cooks is a welcoming place for parents and caregivers to meet, share their parenting concerns, and build community. Our Parent Connection is an interactive, participant-focused program that offers a variety of supports to families — including Mother Goose, Nobody’s Perfect, tips on parenting, nutrition, budgeting, and car seats.

At the heart of the program is our Community Kitchen — a teaching kitchen where participants cook together, enjoy a hot lunch, and prepare nutritious meals to take home for a nominal fee. Participants also receive information on food storage and preparation, prenatal and postnatal vitamins, car seats, weekly food certificates for pregnant women, and many other community resources.

What’s Included
Hands-on teaching kitchen — cook together, share a hot lunch
Take-home nutritious meals for a nominal fee
Food storage, preparation, and nutrition education
Prenatal and postnatal vitamins
Car seat information and safety resources
Weekly food certificates for pregnant women
Connection to other parenting programs and community resources
Call: (250) 498-2538
For Parents & Little Ones

Mother Goose

Mother Goose is a warm, welcoming group experience for parents and children ages 18 months to 3 years. Together, we explore the pleasure and power of using rhymes, songs, and stories to create positive family patterns and give children healthy early experiences with language and communication.

The program strengthens the bond between parent and child, builds confidence in caregivers, and gives little ones a joyful introduction to language, rhythm, and connection — all at no cost.

Program Details
For parents and children ages 18 months – 3 years
Group format featuring rhymes, songs, and stories
Builds early language and communication skills
Strengthens the parent-child bond
Free — call to find out about current scheduling
Call: (250) 498-2538
International Parenting Strategy

Circle of Security

Circle of Security is an internationally recognized parenting workshop that helps parents and caregivers learn to read their children’s cues and respond in ways that build secure, trusting relationships.

By learning the patterns of connection that children naturally seek, parents are able to enhance their parenting style, strengthen attachment, and help their children grow up feeling safe, understood, and confident.

What You’ll Learn
How to recognize and respond to your child’s cues
How to enhance your parenting style and approach
How to build secure attachment and strong relationships
Internationally recognized, evidence-based strategy
Free — call to find out about upcoming workshops
Call: (250) 498-2538
Empathy & Emotional Literacy

Roots of Empathy

Roots of Empathy is an evidence-based classroom program that has helped children develop empathy and emotional literacy in 12 countries. As a leader in the empathy movement, it is backed by research demonstrating lasting, positive impact on children and classrooms.

At the heart of every program is a baby — a tiny teacher who helps children learn about their own feelings and emotions by observing the growing relationship between baby and parent throughout the school year. Research shows Roots of Empathy children demonstrate increased prosocial behaviour, more caring classrooms, a greater understanding of parenting and infants, and reduced aggression.

Proven Outcomes
Increase in prosocial behaviour among participating children
More caring, connected classrooms
Greater understanding of parenting and infants
Decrease in aggression and bullying
Delivered in 12 countries — internationally proven
Call: (250) 498-2538
One-on-One Support

Family Support Services

Family Support Services provides individualized, practical support for families navigating challenges at home. A Desert Sun family support worker can work with you to develop parenting strategies, connect you to community resources, and help build stability for your family.

Support can be delivered in your home or at one of our offices — wherever works best for your family. We meet you where you are, without judgment.

How to Access
Call: (250) 498-2538
In-home and office-based support available
Personalized to your family’s situation
Free and confidential — no referral required
Specialized Support

Parenting After Violence

For families who have experienced family violence, parenting comes with unique challenges. This specialized program supports parents and children in rebuilding safety, strengthening their relationship, and healing together.

Parent Support

For Caregivers

Support for parents who have been affected by family violence, helping them understand how violence impacts children and develop strategies for healing and rebuilding safe family dynamics.

  • Understanding the impact of violence on children
  • Rebuilding safety and routine at home
  • Strengthening the parent-child relationship
  • Call: (250) 498-2538

Child & Youth Support

For Children

Age-appropriate support for children and youth who have witnessed or experienced family violence, helping them process their experiences and build resilience in a safe and supportive environment.

  • Trauma-informed support for children of all ages
  • Coordinated with child & youth counselling team
  • Works alongside the parent support component
  • Call: (250) 498-2538
Child Safety

Supervised Visitation & Exchange

Desert Sun provides a safe, neutral location for court-ordered parent-child visits and custody exchanges. Our trained staff are on-site to ensure the safety and wellbeing of children and all parties involved.

This service is for families where a judge or court has ordered that visits or custody exchanges must be supervised or take place in a neutral setting. All scheduling is arranged through our office.

To Schedule a Visit
Call our Oliver office: (250) 498-2538
Court order required for access to this service
All arrangements made through Desert Sun staff
Both parties must comply with agreed conditions
