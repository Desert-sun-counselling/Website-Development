How We Work

Counselling at Desert Sun

Our approach is trauma-informed, client-centred, and confidential. We work with you at your own pace, in a safe and non-judgmental environment. We also offer Fee for Service counselling for people whose needs cannot be met within our Ministry of Justice mandate.

Free & Confidential

All counselling at Desert Sun is provided free of charge. Your privacy is protected. What you share with your counsellor stays between you.

No Referral Required

You can reach out to us directly to access counselling — no doctor’s referral or MCFD involvement needed. Simply call or email us to get started.

For Men

Men’s Counselling

Desert Sun offers individual and group counselling specifically for men who are working to improve their relationships, manage stress, reduce conflict, and develop healthier patterns of communication and behaviour.

Counselling is a sign of strength, not weakness. Our counsellors provide a safe, non-judgmental space where men can explore what’s getting in the way of the life and relationships they want.

How to Get Started
Email: menscounselling@desertsun.ca
Or call: (250) 498-2538
Individual and group options available
Free and confidential — no referral required

What Men’s Counselling Can Help With

Relationship Challenges

Communication, conflict, separation, and building healthier connections with partners, family, and others.

Stress & Anger

Managing stress, understanding anger responses, and developing healthier coping strategies.

Personal Growth

Exploring patterns of behaviour, past experiences, and building the life you want.

For Women

Stopping the Violence Counselling

Free counselling for women age 19 and over addressing a range of challenges including childhood abuse, domestic violence, adult relationship trauma, and sexualized violence. Our counsellors are trained in trauma-informed approaches and provide a safe, respectful space to work toward healing at your own pace.

You do not need to have experienced crisis or violence to access counselling. If you are struggling — whatever the reason — we are here to help.

How to Get Started
Email: STVoliver2@desertsun.ca
Or call: (250) 498-2538
Open to women age 19 and older
Free and confidential — no referral required
For Young People

Child & Youth Counselling (PEACE Program)

Free counselling for children and youth ages 3 to 18 who have been exposed to intense family conflict or emotional abuse. Delivered in office and school-based settings, we meet young people where they are — literally and figuratively — working in partnership with School District 53 and the Osoyoos Indian Band to provide the best possible support.

Individual Counselling

Ages 3–18

One-on-one counselling tailored to the age, needs, and communication style of each child or youth. Our counsellors use play-based approaches for younger children and more direct methods for older youth.

  • Open to children and youth ages 3 to 18
  • Offered in both Osoyoos and Oliver offices and in SD53 and OIB
  • Play-based therapy available for younger children
  • Parent involvement encouraged where appropriate
  • Call: (250) 498-2538

School-Based Counselling

In-School

Desert Sun counsellors work inside local schools through partnerships with School District 53 and the Osoyoos Indian Band, making it easier for children and youth to access support in a familiar environment.

  • Delivered within SD53 and OIB schools
  • Removes barriers to accessing support
  • Coordinated with school staff where needed
  • Referrals through school counsellors accepted
