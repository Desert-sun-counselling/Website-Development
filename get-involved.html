Volunteer with Desert Sun

Desert Sun enormously values the dedication of our individual and group volunteers. Due to the sensitive nature of much of the work we do, volunteers work in specific roles where they can make the greatest difference while the privacy and wellbeing of clients is fully protected.

In 2024 / 2025, our 16 volunteers contributed 4,252 hours of service to the South Okanagan community. Those hours represent real difference in people’s lives.

Volunteer Role Descriptions

We have a range of meaningful volunteer opportunities across our programs. Explore the roles below and reach out to let us know where you’d like to help.

1. Community Kitchen Cooking Volunteer

Family Support
About the Role

Our Community Kitchen is a warm, welcoming space where volunteers come together to support moms and their children. As a Cooking Volunteer, you will be an integral part of a caring team that nurtures both bodies and spirits.

What You Will Do
  • Assist with preparing and cooking nutritious meals for moms and their children in the Community Kitchen
  • Provide light childcare support, helping to keep young children safe and engaged while their moms participate in the program
  • Help with cleanup after meals, including washing dishes, wiping down surfaces, and leaving the kitchen tidy
  • Work collaboratively and warmly alongside other volunteers and staff to create a welcoming environment
What We Are Looking For
  • A caring and patient nature, especially around young children
  • Basic cooking skills or a willingness to learn
  • Reliability and a commitment to showing up for scheduled kitchen sessions

2. Social Meals Volunteer

Seniors
About the Role

Sharing a meal is one of the most powerful ways to build connection. As a Social Meals Volunteer, you will help prepare and serve meals to seniors and take the time to truly connect with the people you are serving.

What You Will Do
  • Assist with cooking and preparing meals for seniors participating in the Social Meals program
  • Help with cleanup after meals, ensuring the space is left clean and ready
  • Socialize and engage in friendly conversation with senior guests
  • Be present and attentive, offering companionship alongside a nourishing meal
What We Are Looking For
  • A friendly, outgoing personality and genuine enjoyment of conversation with older adults
  • Punctuality and dependability
  • Empathy and respect for seniors of all backgrounds

3. Friendly Visitor Volunteer

Companionship
About the Role

As a Friendly Visitor Volunteer, you will be matched with a senior and commit to building a genuine, ongoing relationship with them through regular visits, shared activities, and simple acts of kindness.

What You Will Do
  • Establish a regular weekly or monthly visit schedule with an assigned senior
  • Build a meaningful personal connection by listening, chatting, and sharing in the senior’s interests and stories
  • Occasionally accompany your senior on outings such as a walk, a trip to the store, or a community event
  • Prepare a simple meal together as a fun and nourishing shared activity when appropriate
  • Refer seniors to other services and supports available in the community when needed
What We Are Looking For
  • A reliable, consistent commitment to visiting your assigned senior on a regular basis
  • Strong listening skills and genuine interest in older adults
  • Respect for boundaries and sensitivity to the senior’s comfort level

4. Volunteer Driver

Driving
About the Role

As a Volunteer Driver, you will provide safe, reliable transportation to medical appointments in Penticton, Kelowna, and surrounding areas for seniors who are unable to use the Community Lift bus service.

What You Will Do
  • Provide safe transportation for seniors traveling to local and out-of-town medical appointments
  • Assist seniors with getting into and out of your vehicle comfortably and safely
  • Offer a friendly, reassuring presence for seniors who may feel anxious about medical visits
  • Coordinate pickup and drop-off times reliably with program staff
What We Are Looking For
  • A valid driver’s licence and a clean driving record
  • A reliable personal vehicle in good working condition
  • Patience and sensitivity when assisting seniors with mobility challenges

Mileage reimbursement is provided for all drives.

5. Fundraising Volunteer

Events & Campaigns
About the Role

As a Fundraising Volunteer, you will bring your organizational skills, creativity, and enthusiasm to help sustain and grow the programs that matter most to our community.

What You Will Do
  • Sit on our Fundraising Committee and contribute to planning discussions and strategy
  • Generate creative ideas for fundraising campaigns, events, and donor engagement
  • Help recruit new donors, supporters, and sponsors from your networks
  • Assist with the planning and execution of fundraising events
What We Are Looking For
  • Highly organized with strong attention to detail
  • Creative thinker who enjoys brainstorming and problem-solving
  • Comfortable reaching out to community members and potential donors
  • Previous fundraising or event planning experience is an asset but not required

Interested in volunteering? We would love to hear from you!
